# Privacy Policy

## 1. Introduction

Thank you for using EasNote ("the App"). We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information, as well as your rights regarding data processing.

This policy complies with international and regional privacy regulations including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

## 2. Data Collection

The App primarily stores your data locally and does not automatically send any personal information to our servers. The information we may collect includes:

### 2.1 Data You Provide Voluntarily
- Note content: Text, images, and other data you create in your notes
- User settings: App preferences and personalized configurations

### 2.2 Data Automatically Collected
- Device information: Device model, operating system version, App version number
- Usage data: Frequency of App feature usage, crash logs (for debugging purposes only)
- Storage permission usage: Only for locally saving notes and images

## 3. Data Usage

We use collected data only for the following purposes:
- Providing and maintaining the core functionality of the App
- Improving App performance and user experience
- Resolving technical issues and App crashes
- Developing new features and optimizing existing ones

## 4. Data Storage and Security

### 4.1 Storage Method
- All note data is stored locally on your device by default
- Data is stored in encrypted form to protect the security of your notes
- The App does not upload data to the cloud unless you explicitly select the backup function (if added)

### 4.2 Security Measures
- Using industry-standard encryption algorithms to protect data
- Regularly updating App security measures
- Limiting App permissions, only requesting necessary functional permissions

## 5. Data Sharing

The App does not share your data with third parties except:
- When required by law or court order
- To protect our rights, property, or safety
- With your explicit consent to share specific data

## 6. User Rights

Under GDPR, CCPA, and other regulations, you have the following rights:

### 6.1 Right of Access
You have the right to access all personal data we store about you.

### 6.2 Right to Rectification
You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.

### 6.3 Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)
You have the right to request deletion of your personal data by simply uninstalling the App or clearing App data.

### 6.4 Right to Data Portability
You have the right to obtain your data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.

### 6.5 Right to Object
You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data.

### 6.6 Right to Restrict Processing
You have the right to restrict our processing of your personal data.

## 7. Permission Explanation

The App requires the following permissions to function properly:

### 7.1 Storage Permission
- Purpose: Save note content and images to local storage
- Scope: Only access the App's private storage area

### 7.2 Camera Permission
- Purpose: Allow you to take and insert photos in notes
- Scope: Only accessed when you use the camera function

### 7.3 Network Permission
- Purpose: Check network connection status (for network monitoring function)
- Scope: Only used for network status detection, no personal data is sent

## 8. Children's Privacy

The App is not intended for children under 13 years of age. If we discover that we have collected data from children under 13, we will delete this information immediately.

## 9. Privacy Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Updated policies will be notified to you within the App and will take effect immediately upon publication.

## 10. Contact Information

If you have any questions or suggestions regarding this Privacy Policy, please contact us through:
- In-App feedback function
- Email: contact@easnote.example.com

## 11. Effective Date

This Privacy Policy is effective as of January 1, 2026.
